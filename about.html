Our Story

Growing voices.
Inspiring futures.

Founded in 2020, Pragnya Young Orators has grown from a small community vision into a thriving speech and debate program where students discover confidence, leadership, and their unique voice.

Founder & Director
Pragnya Young Orators

Meet the Founder

Ragasudha Eripilla

Founder & Director

"I started Pragnya because I believed every student deserves a space to find their voice, grow their confidence, and realize their potential."

Ragasudha founded Pragnya Cultural Society with a simple but powerful vision — to create meaningful change in her community by empowering young students to speak up, lead boldly, and contribute to the world around them.

Her own journey through Toastmasters and competitive speech shaped her understanding of how communication transforms lives. Seeing a gap in opportunities for students to develop real-world communication and leadership skills, she built Pragnya from the ground up — designing programs that blend cultural pride, creative expression, and personal growth.

Her mission has always been to inspire the next generation to believe in themselves, to be curious, compassionate, and courageous — and to know that they have the power to change their community, one voice at a time.

"Change starts with one person who dares to begin."

Our Mission

What drives everything we do

To empower students with confidence, communication, leadership, and competitive excellence through speech and debate.

Pragnya Young Orators is dedicated to helping students develop confident communication, leadership, and critical thinking skills through structured coaching, mentorship, and tournament experiences.

Students explore new ideas, research real issues, and think critically about the world.
Speak with Confidence

Lead with Kindness
We nurture empathetic leaders who lift others and contribute to their communities.
Grow with Purpose
Every program is designed with meaningful outcomes that last far beyond the classroom.

Our Philosophy

How we approach learning

Student-Led Learning
Students take ownership of their growth and lead their own learning journeys.
Growth Mindset
Mistakes are opportunities. Every round, every speech makes you better.
Collaboration
We build each other up — teammates, not just competitors.
Mentorship
Senior students mentor newer members, creating a culture of giving back.
Encouragement Over Pressure
Students thrive in supportive environments. We celebrate effort, not just outcomes.
